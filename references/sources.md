# AI News Sources

Use this reference only when you need source selection guidance or search starting points.

## Source Tiers

### Tier 1: Primary sources

Check these first when the story is about a specific company, lab, or institution.

- Company blogs and newsroom pages: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Google DeepMind, Microsoft, Meta, NVIDIA, Amazon AWS, Hugging Face, Mistral, xAI, Stability AI, Runway, Perplexity
- China AI companies when relevant: Alibaba Cloud or Qwen, Baidu, Tencent, ByteDance, Zhipu, Moonshot AI, MiniMax
- Research organizations: university lab blogs, arXiv paper pages, benchmark leaderboards, official GitHub releases
- Public institutions: White House, NIST, European Commission, EU AI Office, UK government, UK AI Safety Institute, FTC, national regulators or courts

### Tier 2: Trusted secondary coverage

Use these to discover stories or confirm impact. Prefer direct reporting over commentary.

- Reuters
- Bloomberg
- TechCrunch
- The Verge
- VentureBeat
- Semafor
- Wired
- Financial Times

### Tier 3: Use sparingly

Use only when they link back to better sources or provide unique context.

- Personal blogs
- Newsletter roundups
- Social posts
- Generic AI content farms

## Query Patterns

Adapt these patterns to the user request and the available search tools.

### Cross-industry scan

- "AI news last 3 days model release funding regulation"
- "site:openai.com OR site:anthropic.com OR site:deepmind.google OR site:ai.meta.com AI announcement"
- "Reuters AI latest OR TechCrunch AI latest OR The Verge AI latest"

### Company-specific scan

- "<company> AI blog latest"
- "site:<official-domain> AI release OR launch OR announcement"
- "<company> AI Reuters"

### Policy and legal scan

- "AI regulation last 7 days Reuters"
- "site:ec.europa.eu AI Act OR AI Office"
- "site:ftc.gov artificial intelligence"

### Research scan

- "<lab name> research blog latest AI"
- "site:arxiv.org LLM OR multimodal OR agent recency"
- "<benchmark or paper title> official release"

## Selection Rules

- Prefer one primary source plus one reputable secondary source for high-impact stories.
- If the same event appears in many outlets, keep the clearest source pair instead of listing duplicates.
- If a story exists only as a repost or rumor, drop it unless the user explicitly wants rumors.
- Avoid paywalled-only sourcing when an accessible equivalent exists.

## Default Digest Scope

When the user does not specify a scope, aim for a balanced mix of:

1. Product and model launches
2. Enterprise and platform updates
3. Funding, M&A, and partnerships
4. Policy, legal, and safety developments
5. Research breakthroughs with likely market impact
